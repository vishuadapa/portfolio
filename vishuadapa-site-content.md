## ABOUT

Twenty-some years in enterprise IT taught me one thing above everything else. Tech problems are rarely about tech. They’re about risk, money, and people, and if you don’t solve for all three you haven’t really solved anything.

I’ve held just about every seat at the table. I started in support, taking the calls when things broke at 2 AM. Then I ran the infrastructure myself: storage, SAN fabrics, large VMware environments, backup at multi-petabyte scale. I lived with the consequences of every architecture decision I made, through migrations and data center consolidations that didn’t have the luxury of going wrong. From there I moved into professional services at Dell EMC, implementing what others had sold. Then into architecture and presales, designing it.

The scale grew with the career. The environment I ran early on was a couple of petabytes across multiple data centers, which was a serious footprint for its time. In professional services I worked across environments measured in tens of petabytes spanning data centers and cloud. As a presales engineer I’ve covered some of the largest environments on the planet, hundreds of petabytes across multiple data centers and cloud. That whole path shapes how I work today as a Senior Systems Engineer. I won’t propose anything I wouldn’t be willing to implement, operate, or get paged for at 2 AM myself.

The last decade has been deep in cyber resilience and hybrid cloud. Recovery vault design, DR strategy, and data protection across AWS, Azure, and GCP for customers in Energy, Financial Services, Healthcare, Insurance, Aerospace Manufacturing, and Retail. I earned my CISSP somewhere in there, because you can’t honestly talk about recovery if you don’t understand the attack.

There’s a pattern to how I work. If I do something twice, I’ll build a tool so nobody has to do it a third time. The latest one is an automated health check framework that looks at customer environments through three lenses, security hardening, capacity, and performance, and turns days of manual review into something my whole team can run in minutes. It’s the same instinct behind the enablement programs I build and the time I spend helping younger engineers learn to think commercially instead of only technically.

Lately I’m just as interested in enterprise AI, and I mean building with it, not posting about it. Agents, automation, real workflows. If you want to compare notes on cloud strategy, cyber resilience, or where AI actually fits in the enterprise, I’m easy to reach.
